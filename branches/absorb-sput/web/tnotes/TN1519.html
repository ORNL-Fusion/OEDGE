Some Ideas for a SOLOpt24A

20 July 2000


It would be valuable to have something simpler, cruder and more robust than either SOL23 or SOL24 to model/prescribe detachment to help us get on the air. Consider the following ideas:


For each flux tube:


  1. Assume values for 4 free parameters : Tt, Lf, dI, dP. As with SOL24, Tt and Lf have to be established by trying to get a best match with Da and/or Dg data. Lf stands for the distance of the ‘ionization front’ from the target at s|| = 0. dI and dP are parameters that will be found in the process of getting a self-consistent solution, i.e. before turning to the expt Da and/or Dg data.

    Note: This definition of Lf is different than corresponding parameter in SOL24, which is Lpeak, the distance from the target at s|| = 0 to the density peak (indeed, the region between Lpeak and Lf is where problems tend to occur at lower temperatures).


  2. We know nu, Tu (for a start let’s take Te = Ti everywhere), thus pu and qin from either having solved the outer (attached) SOL successfully, or from the upstream Thomson. Assume qin = 7/2 ko Tu7/2/L.


  3. Take Is+, i.e. Gt, from inside LPs.


  4. There’s now enough info to calculate qt and thus the volumetric power loss in the tube. There’s also enough info to calculate p­t and thus the volumetric momentum loss in the tube.


  5. We also know the particle flux density to the target, Gt. Initially assume no volumetric recombination and assume the particle source in the tube equals the target loss. Assume this source constant over Lf – dI < s|| < Lf + dI. (Note we do not therefore use EIRENE.)


  6. Assume the vol mom sink uniform over 0 < s|| < Lf.


  7. Assume the vol power sink uniform over Lf - dP < s|| < Lf + dP.


  8. For initial set of assumed values of Tt, Lf, dI, dP apply SOL22 to 0 < s|| < L.


  9. Now we know what p(Lf) should be, namely equal to pu.


  10. And we know what T(Lf + dP) should be, namely:


    T(Lf + dP)7/2 = Tu7/2 – 7/2 qin(L – Lf – dP)/ ko.


    (Admittedly if dI > dP then q||conv should also be involved in calculating T(Lf + dP), and not just q||cond, but this is a small effect and we may ignore it.)

    Adjust the values of dI and dP in order to get matches to p(Lf) and T(Lf + dP). We have 2 constraints to establish 2 unknowns. At the same time one has to find a way of iterating regarding the particle source, which equals the loss to the targets plus the volume recomb on the flux tube, and one can only calculate the latter once the plasma solution is known.

Potential Advantages


  1. No use of EIRENE, so may be fast.


  2. No risk that vol recomb will dig a “T-hole”, since T(s||) is assured to be mono increasing.


  3. Smooth n(s||), T(s||) all the way from target to target is assured.


  4. Each flux tube is analyzed independently of each other. No coupling via EIRENE.
